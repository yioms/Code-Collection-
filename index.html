West Philippine Sea

The West Philippine Sea is a body of water in the western
Pacific Ocean, bordered by the Philippines to the east
and south, Vietnam to the west and north, China to the
north and northwest, and Taiwan to the northeast. It is
home to numerous islands, reefs, shoals and other land
features, including the disputed Spratly Islands which
are claimed wholly or partially by China, the Philippines,
Vietnam, Malaysia, Brunei and Taiwan.

The complex disputes over the ownership of the Spratly
Islands have been a major point of contention in the
region, leading to tensions and conflicts between some
countries that claim sovereignty over the same land mass.
The West Philippine Sea, rich in natural resources such
as fish and oil, has also been a site of increasing
American military presence in the area, as well as a
heightened geopolitical interest from other nations
such as Japan and Australia.

The Philippines has filed a case in 2013 at the Permanent
Court of Arbitration against China's territorial claims
within the nine-dash line over much of the West Philippine
Sea, arguing that it violates the United Nations Convention
on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS).
